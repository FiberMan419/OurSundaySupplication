Our Sunday Supplication

Sunday, June 21, 2020

Solemnity of the Body and Blood of Christ

Holy Mass is at the very core of our worship as a Catholic community. Nothing can ever be substituted for the celebration of the Eucharist. But in this exceptional time of difficulty for our communities, our nation, and even the whole world, this order of prayer can be offered by individuals and families and engages us in our Sunday celebration of the Word of God. We can pray this prayer in our homes—the “domestic churches” which help build up the Body of Christ on earth—and thus be connected to the entire Church at prayer.

During this time of prayer, we include the opportunity to make a Spiritual Communion, since we are presently unable to receive Holy Communion. Making a Spiritual Communion is a worthy practice, recommended by innumerable saints throughout the ages, which unites our whole selves to God and his Church. For, despite not being able to celebrate Mass together, this is certainly not a time to abandon the praise and supplication of almighty God, for he never abandons us! Now is the time to love and serve the Lord as best we can in our limited capacities.

A reading from the book of Prophet Jeremiah (Jeremiah 20:10-13)

Jeremiah said: “I hear the whisperings of many: ‘Terror on every side! Denounce! let us denounce him!’ All those who were my friends are on the watch for any misstep of mine. ‘Perhaps he will be trapped; then we can prevail, and take our vengeance on him.’ But the LORD is with me, like a mighty champion: my persecutors will stumble, they will not triumph. In their failure they will be put to utter shame, to lasting, unforgettable confusion. O LORD of hosts, you who test the just, who probe mind and heart, let me witness the vengeance you take on them, for to you I have entrusted my cause. The Gospel of the Lord.
―Praise to you, Lord Jesus Christ

For Reflection:

  1. “Testifying to the Spirit of truth” – bearing witness to the Gospel – takes courage given to us by the Holy Spirit, especially when the message we are asked to share may not be well-received by others. Have you ever felt like Jeremiah, mocked and derided for testifying to the Spirit of truth? What was the result of your testimony? Was it worth the challenge and effort, and why?
  2. Have you ever avoided bearing witness to the Gospel out of fear of what others might say or think of you? What did you learn from this experience? What may God, who is rich in mercy, be saying to you now as you reflect upon this experience?
  3. How has the experience of not receiving the Eucharist during these months impacted your relationship with God?
  4. In the Gospel, Jesus affirms our eternal destiny and tells us, “Do not be afraid.” What is Jesus be calling you to witness to right now – in thought, word, or action? What fears or challenges must you overcome to answer his call?
